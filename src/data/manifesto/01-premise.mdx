Mark Fisher defines the Gothic flatline: *a plane where it is no longer possible to differentiate the animate from the inanimate and where to have agency is not necessarily to be alive.*

elsewhere: *the flatline is where everything happens, the Other Side, behind or beyond the screens (of subjectivity), the site or primary process where identity is produced (and dismantled): the 'line Outside.'*

in the relentless encoding and overcoding of our planet, beneath a great homogenization, beyond its aspirations of total ecocide, ecology maintains. nature returns from the dead as anti-object: that which overcomes itself and anorganically lives, in a manner totally indifferent to "life."

in digital archives and dead air, decayed blueprints and decrepit radio towers, the flatline underground seeks communication with this life.
