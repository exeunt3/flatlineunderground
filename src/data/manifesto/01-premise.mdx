Mark Fisher defines the Gothic flatline: *a plane where it is no longer possible to differentiate the animate from the inanimate and where to have agency is not necessarily to be alive.*

elsewhere: *the flatline is where everything happens, the Other Side, behind or beyond the screens (of subjectivity), the site or primary process where identity is produced (and dismantled): the 'line Outside.'*

in the relentless encoding and overcoding of our planet, beneath a great homogenization, a flatlined ecology maintains. behind the screen of global conquest, nature returns from the dead as anti-object: that which overcomes itself and anorganically lives, as an endless aberration in the zeros and ones.

in digital archives and dead air, decayed blueprints and decrepit radio towers, the flatline underground seeks communication with these cybernetic accidents.
